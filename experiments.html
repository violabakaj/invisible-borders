Physics-Based Demonstrations of Pollution Control

“Invisible Borders” includes two core demonstrative models that explain how environmental pollution spreads and how it can be reduced through physical pathway control.

1. Air Pathway Demonstration

Vapor Capture & Multi-Stage Treatment Model

Objective: To demonstrate how polluted vapors can be intercepted at the source before spreading toward nearby residential areas.

Physical Principle: Advection, turbulence, and airflow structure determine dispersion distance and exposure risk.

  • Wind velocity
  • Turbulence structure
  • Distance from source

Key Insight: Transport control is most efficient when applied at or near the emission source.

2. Water Pathway Demonstration

Multi-Layer Filtration Column System

Objective: To demonstrate how layered filtration reduces pollutant mobility in water pathways.

Physical Principle: Flow velocity, permeability, and residence time govern retention efficiency.

  • Flow velocity
  • Residence time
  • Surface interaction area

Key Insight: Increasing contact time improves pollutant interception.

Integrated Insight

Environmental risk can be reduced by controlling transport pathways in air and water before pollutants spread widely.
